Classic Kimbap

Heres a step-by-step guide on how to make a classic Korean kimbap with seasoned rice, beef, and lots of vegetables.

Kimbap, sometimes spelled gimbap, is a rice, meat—usually beef—and vegetables rolled in roasted seaweed. During my chlidhood, when we took famliy road trips in our VW Hatchback, my mom would always pack kimbap in a huge Tupperware container and put it in our old Igloo cooler, along with bottles of Coke. We never stopped at gas stations or restaurants for food. We pulled over at rest stops, fliled our bellies with kimbap and a Coke, and we were set!

Ingredients

for the steak
  • 6 ounces skirt steak or sirloin beef, cut into 3 x 1/4-inch strips
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, grated
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oli
For the rice
  • 4 cups freshly cooked medium-grain white rice
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oli
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
For the spinach
  • 8 ounces spinach leaves, washed
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oli
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
